Development of Generative AI

History of Generative Ai is quite short, introduced in the 1960s originally in the form of chatbots (Foote, 2024). Revealing that the
technology emerging recently is not completely accurate, only in this decade did it evolve rapidly (Toloka, 2024).

Since late 2022, genAI has been coming out with new variations of its technology several times in a month
(What’s the Future of Generative AI? An Early View in 15 Charts, 2023).

High-quality text, images, videos, audio and synthetic data can currently be created under a minute (Foote, 2024).

TIMELINE

EARLY CONCEPTION OF AI

Early 20th century

  • Scientists from various specialties were involved in
    the research of artificial intelligence(Toloka, 2024).

1944

  • The first neural network models were developed by Neuropsychologist and mathematician,
    Warren McCullough and Walter Pitts respectively ( Dhamani, 2024)

Since 1941 or earlier

  • Famous mathematician Alan Turing, was tasked with
    the difficulty of artificial intelligence (Toloka, 2024).

1950

  • To test the machine of intelligence, the “imitation game”
    was set out by Alan Turing ( Dhamani, 2024)

1952

  • Arthur Samuel, who coined the phrase “machine learning”, developed
    the first machine learning algorithm for playing checkers (Foote, 2024).

BIRTH OF AI

1956

  • Artificial network workshop, Dartmouth Summer Research, was held. Researchers specialising in
    various sciences debated the potential of computing machines (Toloka, 2024).
  • Suggestion of a new academic discipline: artificial intelligence was made among many
    by a computer scientist, John McCarthy (Toloka, 2024).

1966

  • A therapist chatbot, ELIZA, was released by Joseph Weizenbaum ( Dhamani, 2024).
    First talking robot, have the ability to communicate with human in a natural language

1970 - 80s

  • Due to Natural Language Processing(NLP), symbolic systems
    were the most popular ( Dhamani, 2024).
  • During this duration, there was less funding and research breakthroughs.
    Resulting in a period afterwards called “AI Winter”. (Dhamani, 2024)

1990s

  • New standards on statistical models were beginning
    to be set on NLP tasks ( Dhamani, 2024)
  • First repetitive and deep neural networks were
    trained ( Dhamani, 2024)

RISE OF AI

1990s and 2000s

  • Computer processing greatly matured (Toloka, 2024)

2006

  • First commercially successful NLP system, Google Translate ( Dhamani, 2024)

2010s

  • Being based on deep learning techniques, generative artificial
    intelligence took off rapidly (Toloka, 2024)

2018

  • GPT-1, first Generative Pre-trained Transformer model,
    is released by OpenAI ( Dhamani, 2024)

2019

  • Google releases BERT, an additional large Transformer model, becoming another
    widely used to create word embeddings or representations to combat concerns about
    misuse after months delay after GPT-2 was launched by OpenAI ( Dhamani, 2024)

2020

  • Release of GPT-3 ( Dhamani, 2024)

2022

  • Launch of ChatGPT by OpenAI, fame was achieved overnight ( Dhamani, 2024)

November 2022

  • OpenAI achieved a new level of artificial intelligence with the combination of
    ChatGPT and its variations, they are known as “smarter chatbots” (Foote, 2024).
    Possessing the ability to research, produce sensible good writing, generate realistic videos,
    audio, and images (Foote, 2024)
